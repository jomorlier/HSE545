Problem 5¶

[10pt] In a heads or tails coin-tossing experiment the probability of occurrence of a head (1) is $q$ and that of a tail (0) is $1-q$. Let $x_i, \, i=1,2,\cdots,m$ be the resulting experimental outcomes, $x_i \in \{0,1\}$, Show that the ML estimate of $q$ is

$$q_{\text{ML}} = \frac{1}{m} \sum_{i=1}^{m}x_i$$

Hint: The likelihood function is

$$P(x_1 x_2 \cdots x_m \mid q) = \prod_{i=1}^{m} q^{x_i}(1-q)^{1-x_i}$$

Problem 6¶

We showed that if the likelihood function is Gaussian with unknowns, the mean $\mu$ as well as the covariance matrix $\Sigma$, then the ML estimates are given by

$$ \begin{align*} \hat \mu_m & = \frac{1}{m} \sum_{i =1}^{m} x_i \\ \hat \Sigma_m & = \frac{1}{m} \sum_{i=1}^{m} (x_i - \hat \mu_m)(x_i - \hat \mu_m)^T \end{align*} $$

1) [10pt] Show that the ML estimates of the mean value and the covariance matrix can be computed recursively, that is,

$$ \begin{align*} \hat \mu_{m+1} &= \hat \mu_m + \frac{1}{m+1} (x_{m+1} - \hat \mu_m)\\ \hat \Sigma_{m+1} & = \frac{m}{m+1} \hat \Sigma_m + \frac{m}{(m+1)^2} (x_{m+1} - \hat \mu_m)(x_{m+1} - \hat \mu_m)^T \end{align*} $$

2) [2pt] Explane why such a recurive method is important when the estimates are implemented.
