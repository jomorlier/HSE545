Problem 1¶

Let $R = R(\theta)$ be a rotation matrix with a rotational angle of $\theta$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$.

  1. Prove that $$R^TR=I \;\text{in}\; \mathbb{R}^n$$

    Hint: Euclidian distances of vectors are preserved after a rotational operation. (i.e., $\lVert Rx \rVert = \lVert x \rVert$ for any $x \in \mathbb{R}^n$ )

  2. Prove that $$R^T(\theta) = R^{-1}(\theta) = R(-\theta)\;\text{in}\;\mathbb{R}^n$$

  3. Show that column vectors in $R$ are orthogonal in $\mathbb{R}^n$.

Solution¶

  1. $\lVert Rx \rVert ^2 = (Rx)^T(Rx) = x^TR^TRx = \lVert x \rVert ^2 = x^Tx\,$ for any $x \implies R^TR = I$

  2. $R^TR = I \implies R^{-1} = R^T$

  3. $R^TR = I \implies r_i^T r_j = \delta_{ij}$

Problem 2¶

  1. Define $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$ as the projection of vector $x$ onto vector $y$. The projection operation is illustrated in Figure 1. Find matrix $P$ when $f(x)=Px$.

  2. Define $g: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$ as the reflection of vector $x$ about vector $y$. The reflection operation is also illustrated in Figure 1. Express matrix $A$ using matrix $P$ when $g(x)=Ax$.

Figure 1

Solution¶

  1. $P = y\left(y^Ty\right)^{-1}y^T$

  2. $g(x) = x + 2(f(x)-x) = 2f(x)-x = (2P-I)x \implies A = 2\left(y\left(y^Ty\right)^{-1}y^T \right) - I$

Problem 3¶

Find solution $D$ of the optimization problem with the following objective function and constraints. Here, $\lVert \cdot \rVert_2$ is the Euclidean norm (or distance).



$$\begin{align*} D= \min_Z \quad & \lVert X_1 - Z \rVert_2 + \lVert X_2 - Z \rVert_2 \\ \\ \text{subject to} \quad & Z \in V=\text{span} \{V_1, V_2\} \end{align*}$$

Figure 2

Solution¶

$D^*$ is the shortest distance from $X_1$ to $X_2$ via $Z$ on the spanned hyperplane. The shortest line is the straight line from $X_1$ and $Y$ which is mirrored reflection of $X_2$ with respect to the hyperplane.

$$ \begin{align*} D^* &= \lVert X_1 - MX_2 \rVert = \lVert X_1 - \left( 2V \left(V^TV \right)^{-1}V^T-I\right)X_2\rVert \\ &=\lVert MX_1 - X_2\rVert \end{align*} $$

where $M$ is mirror transformation and $V = [V_1 \; V_2]$

Problem 5¶

The regularized least-squares problem has the form


$$ \min_{\theta} \;\lVert A\theta -y\rVert_2^2 + \lambda \lVert \theta \rVert_2^2$$

  1. Show that the solution is given by

    $$ \hat{\theta} = \left( A^T A + \lambda I_n \right)^{-1} A^T y $$ (Do not use the method of Lagrangian multipliers)

  2. Find and draw an approximated curve of the given data points in either Matlab or python
    • overcome overfitting
    • use 10 RBFs with $\sigma = 1$
    • choose a proper value of $\lambda$ on your own

Solution¶

$$ \begin{align*} \left\Vert A\theta - y\big\Vert^2_{2} + \big\Vert \sqrt{\lambda}I\theta-0\right\Vert^2_{2} & = \Bigg\Vert \begin{bmatrix} A\\ \sqrt{\lambda}I \end{bmatrix} \theta- \begin{bmatrix} y\\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \Bigg\Vert^2_{2} \end{align*}$$$$ \hat{\theta} = \left( \begin{bmatrix} A\\ \sqrt{\lambda}I \end{bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} A\\ \sqrt{\lambda}I \end{bmatrix}\right)^{-1}\begin{bmatrix} A\\ \sqrt{\lambda}I \end{bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} y\\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \left( A^T A + \lambda I_n \right)^{-1} A^T y $$
